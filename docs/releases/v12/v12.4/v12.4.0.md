## Description of Changes

<!-- user-editable-section-start -->
- Accept `node-version` as an input to all workflows.
  - This is the first step in allowing my projects to start using Node 26.
  - For now the default has been set to 22 to preserve backwards compatibility.

## Additional Notes

- Yep, 26 recently got released and it enables Temporal by default! We all know what means...
- Yeah, that's right! Goodbye JavaScript Date class, you will ~~not~~ be missed.
- Gone soon will be the days of having to add 1 to the month just because someone decided it'd be a good idea to zero-index it.
- Somewhat gone will also be the days where adding a month to the current date produces weird results at the month boundaries (trust me, I **know** that pain!)
- But not gone will be the days where we have to deal with humanity's bs decision to make us adjust our clocks every March and October, unfortunately...
